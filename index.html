Vision

ProofX compresses time-to-evidence for mathematical and formal systems work. We fuse deterministic ML/Python execution with theorem-prover rigor and SRE-grade observability so teams move from idea → experiment → verified artifact without ambiguity.

  • Mission: turn "proof of concept" into proof with provenance — every run replayable, every claim inspectable.
  • Why it matters: as AI increases conjecture velocity, reproducibility and traceability become existential for trust.
  • Who it serves: research labs, critical-infrastructure operators, and public-interest math & verification projects.

Open-source non-profit; not SaaS. Read the long-form thesis at vision.proofx.org.

The problem

  • Brittle, opaque proof workflows; hard to reproduce across machines and versions.
  • Noisy search; counterexamples are hard to surface, label, and track over time.
  • Insufficient audit-grade traces and seeds; peer review and incident response drag.
  • Latency to verified evidence blocks iteration, collaboration, and external confidence.

Context: our open-source theorem prover comparison frames where each tool excels and how ProofX augments workflows.

What ProofX makes possible

  • Deterministic portfolios across SMT/FOL/FMC with SAT bridges, typed obligations, replayable seeds.
  • Entropy-aware scheduling + curriculum runs prioritize promising paths and surface outliers early.
  • First-class telemetry: traces, dashboards, anomaly flags, decay maps, run diffs (audit-ready).
  • Truth-aligned latency: shortest path from conjecture to inspectable evidence and publishable artifacts.
  • Backed by: Deterministic Portfolios · FMC bridges · Replay Manifests.

Output: witnesses, notebooks, and manifests pinned in an artifact registry; every attempt becomes a data point in a growing evaluation wall.

FAQ

How does the architecture ensure determinism and traceability?
  • Layered solver portfolios (SMT/FOL/FMC) with fixed seeds and versioned configs.
  • Run manifests capture seeds, routes, and parameters for byte-for-byte replay.
  • Traces & diffs at step and run level; obligations + witnesses are first-class artifacts.
What's the reliability moat?
  • Seeded runs + portfolio routing with backpressure under load.
  • Artifact registry for witnesses, notebooks, manifests, and structured traces.
  • Compounding corpus that improves scheduling priors and is hard to replicate.
  • Security-first deploys: private cloud, on-prem, and air-gapped modes.
Is this a SaaS?

No. ProofX is an open-source non-profit. We collaborate via research partnerships, pilots, and operator support. See press.proofx.org for positioning and valuation method.

Deployment & security posture?
  • Reproducible containers with pinned dependencies and verified builds.
  • Strict retention + minimal telemetry modes for regulated environments.
  • Access controls suitable for classified or air-gapped sites.
Truth-aligned latency: how is time-to-evidence minimized?
  • Entropy-aware & curriculum scheduling; caching and portfolio tuning.
  • Operator dashboards shrink MTTR for failures and anomalies.
  • Counterexample harvesting feeds back into priors and test suites.
