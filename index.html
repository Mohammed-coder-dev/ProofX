Vision

ProofX compresses time-to-evidence. We engineer proof-grade reliability by unifying deterministic ML/Python execution with entropy-aware search and audit-grade telemetry.

Open-source non-profit; not SaaS. Built for labs, operators, and public-interest research. Read the long-form thesis at vision.proofx.org.

The problem

  • Proof workflows are brittle, opaque, and slow to reproduce.
  • Search is noisy; counterexamples are hard to surface and track.
  • Teams lack audit-grade traces across runs and versions.
  • Latency to verified evidence blocks iteration and trust.

What ProofX makes possible

  • Deterministic portfolios across SMT/FOL/FMC with SAT bridges.
  • Entropy-aware scheduling + curriculum runs to find needles fast.
  • First-class telemetry: traces, dashboards, anomaly flags, decay maps.
  • Truth-aligned latency: shortest path from conjecture to evidence.

FAQ

How does the architecture ensure determinism and traceability?
  • Deterministic solvers layered with symbolic search and first-class observability.
  • Seeded randomness + versioned runs; identical replay across machines and time.
  • Typed obligations with witness artifacts; every attempt becomes part of an evaluation wall.
What’s the reliability moat?
  • Seeded runs + portfolio routing with backpressure under load.
  • Artifact registry for witnesses, notebooks, and traces.
  • Compounding corpus of structured traces that’s hard to replicate.
  • Security-first deploys: private cloud, on-prem, and air-gapped modes.
Is this a SaaS?

No. ProofX is an open-source non-profit. We collaborate via research partnerships, pilots, and operator support. See press.proofx.org for positioning and valuation method.

Deployment & security posture?
  • On-prem and air-gapped ready; minimal-telemetry modes with strict retention.
  • Reproducible containers; seeds + manifests for verified builds.
  • Access controls suited for regulated & classified environments.
Truth-aligned latency: how is time-to-evidence minimized?
  • Entropy-aware scheduling, curriculum runs, and counterexample harvesting.
  • Operator dashboards, anomaly flags, and decay maps for rapid iteration.
  • Portfolio selection + caching to route compute where it matters.
