FAQ

How does ProofX ensure determinism and traceability?

ProofX portfolios are seeded, versioned, and replayable with byte-for-byte manifests.

What is the reliability moat?

ProofX builds compounding corpora of runs and counterexamples that improve priors and are hard to replicate.

Is ProofX a SaaS?

No. ProofX is an open-source non-profit, structured around research partnerships and operator deployments.

Deployment posture?

Containers, pinned builds, strict retention modes.

How does ProofX minimize time-to-evidence?

Entropy-aware scheduling and replay manifests.

What does replay guarantee?

Manifested seeds, routes, and configs guarantee byte-identical runs across environments.

How are counterexamples handled?

Counterexamples are harvested, labeled, and pushed into a witness registry for review and regression.

What is the observability model?

Trace capture at step/run levels, anomaly flags, and audit-ready exports.

How are portfolios tuned?

Deterministic routing with adaptive backpressure under load.

What environments are supported?

Private cloud, on-prem, and air-gapped deployments.

Does ProofX replace existing provers?

ProofX orchestrates and augments existing provers with deterministic control and telemetry.

How are artifacts stored?

Artifacts are pinned in a registry with provenance metadata.

What is the license model?

Open-source non-profit with research and operator collaboration pathways.

What is the roadmap focus?

Scaling reproducibility, richer parity analysis, and curriculum scheduling.

How does ProofX support peer review?

Replayable manifests and exportable witnesses reduce review friction.

How is security handled?

Minimal telemetry modes, strict retention, and role-scoped access controls.

What about performance regressions?

Run diffs and decay maps surface regressions and trigger triage.

What integrations exist?

APIs for solver backends, artifact registries, and dashboards.

How is success measured?

Latency to verifiable evidence, replay fidelity, and defect discovery rate.
